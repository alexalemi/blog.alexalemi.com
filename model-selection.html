Model Selection, the three families

Alexander A. Alemi. 2022-06-08

How do we choose between hypotheses?

The way I see it there are broadly three different families of model selection criterion, there are those that focus on prediction, those that focus on correctness and those that strike a balance between the two.

Prediction

A lot of the time the only thing we really care about is how well our model can make future predictions. If the true data distribution is some $\nu(x)$ and we have a model $p(x)$ a very natural way to score the effectiveness of the model is to measure the KL divergence between the two.

AIC, WAIC, Cross validation, all that.

Correctness

BIC, marginal likelihood, free energy, all that.

In between

MDL, ELBO, things like that.
